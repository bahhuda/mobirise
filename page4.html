MADRASAH ALJUNIED AL-ISLAMIAH

Al-Junied History

Madrasah Aljunied Al-Islamiah A Tradition of Excellence Forging into the Future. Since its inception in 1927, Madrasah Aljunied Al-Islamiah has set for itself a tradition of academic excellence. Its reputation is known not only in Singapore, but also throughout Southeast Asia.

It is with this excellence that Aljunied graduates earn recognition and acceptance by such esteemed institutions as the the University of Al-Azhar in Cairo.

The Madrasah also prides itself in a number of its alumni who had completed their studies in Al-Azhar University and serve their nations. Amongst them are, Pehin Dato Seri Utama Dr Haji Mohamad Zain Haji Serudin, now Minister for Religious Affairs In Brunei Darussalam, several grand Muftis, scholars and religious leaders of Singapore, Sabah, Sarawak, Pahang and Brunei Darussalam, and a number of lecturers at the universities in this region. 

The opening of the Madrasah in 1927 represented the pride of Islam and the Muslim community. With the grace of Allah, Sayyid Abdur Rahman bin Junied bin Umar bin Ali Aljunied built the Madrasah from the endowment funds of his grandfather, Sayyid Umar bin Ali Aljunied, the founder of Waqf Aljunied.

From a plot of burial land to an institution of Islamic learning, that could have summed up the history of Madrasah Aljunied Al-Islamiah. Over the years, many events have taken place to contribute to its current prestigious standing. Dedicated teachers and excellent curricula, both secular and religious, have made it the premier school of Islamic learning in Singapore.
